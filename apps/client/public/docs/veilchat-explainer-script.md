# VeilChat — Full Explainer Video Script

**Estimated runtime:** ~6:20 minutes at a steady, conversational pace (~150 words per minute).
**Total word count:** ~1,150 words.
**Recommended voice direction:** Warm, measured, confident — the calm authority of a documentary narrator (think *Hidden Brain*, *99% Invisible*, or Apple's product films). Slow enough to breathe; never rushed.
**Recommended music:** Sparse ambient pad in a major key — light strings or warm synth. Builds gently through the features chapter, pulls back during the honest analysis, returns warm for the close.
**Brand palette:** #FCF5EB cream · #2E6F40 forest green · #253D2C dark green · #CFFFDC pale green · #3C5A47 muted green text.

---

## Chapter 1 — Cold Open (0:00 – 0:25)

**Visual:** Dark green to cream gradient. A single padlock icon morphs slowly into a chat bubble. Slow zoom-in.

**Voiceover:**
> Every day, you have private conversations. The kind you'd never want a stranger reading. The kind you trust to one person — the person on the other end of the message.
>
> But on most messaging apps you use today, you're not the only one in the room.

---

## Chapter 2 — The Problem (0:25 – 1:05)

**Visual:** Phone-shaped frame showing several familiar messenger logos. Subtle red privacy alerts appear above each one — "shares contact graph," "logs metadata," "linked to phone number," "tied to identity."

**Voiceover:**
> Big-name messengers say they're private — and many of them do encrypt your messages. But they still know who you talk to, when, how often, where from, and what apps you opened before you typed.
>
> That information has a name. It's called metadata. And metadata is the most damaging intelligence about you that exists. It's how governments, advertisers, and bad actors build a complete picture of your life — without ever reading a single word.

---

## Chapter 3 — Introducing VeilChat (1:05 – 1:40)

**Visual:** VeilChat logo reveal — the leaf-checkmark mark scales in from the center on a soft cream background. Tagline appears below: *"Message privately. Built for the people you actually trust."*

**Voiceover:**
> This is VeilChat. A privacy-first messenger built for people who actually believe their conversations are no one else's business.
>
> It's calm. It's beautifully simple. It's free, it always will be, and it's open source — meaning anyone can read, audit, and verify exactly what it does and what it doesn't do.
>
> You don't need a phone number. You don't need to give us your email. You don't need to trust us — because we built it so you don't have to.

---

## Chapter 4 — Core Features (1:40 – 3:25)

**Visual:** A series of feature cards animate into view, one after the other. Each card has a small green icon and a short label. The phone mockup from the landing page sits to one side, demonstrating each feature as it's spoken.

**Voiceover:**
> Here's what's in the box.
>
> **End-to-end encryption — by default.** On every message, every voice call, every photo, every file. Not as an opt-in setting. Not for some chats. For all of them. The keys to read your messages live on your device and your friend's device, and nowhere else. Not on our servers. Not in our backups. Not anywhere.
>
> **No phone number required.** You sign up with a username. That's it. There's no number for someone to look you up with, no number that ties your messages to your real-world identity.
>
> **Disappearing messages.** Pick a timer — five seconds, an hour, a week — and your message is gone from both devices when it expires. No screenshots. No permanent record.
>
> **Verified contacts.** Tap a friend's name and you can compare a small safety code with them — in person, on a call, in another app. If the codes match, you know the conversation is secured between exactly the two of you. Nobody is in the middle.
>
> **Group chats with the same protections. Voice and video calls that travel encrypted. Photos and voice notes that stay between you.** Everything works the same way: locked, by default, end to end.
>
> **Works on every device.** Phone, tablet, laptop. Android, iOS, web, desktop. Install it as a progressive web app from your browser in two seconds — no store account, no review queue.

---

## Chapter 5 — How It Works (3:25 – 4:30)

**Visual:** A simple architecture diagram draws itself on screen. A device icon on the left, a device icon on the right, and an opaque "VeilChat server" box in the middle. Animated dotted lines show an encrypted message traveling through, with a clear "🔒 unreadable here" label over the server.

**Voiceover:**
> Here's what's happening behind the scenes when you press send.
>
> Your message is encrypted on your device using a key that only you and the person you're talking to share. The encrypted message — meaningless to everyone else — travels through our servers to your friend's device, where their key decrypts it.
>
> We never see the message. We never see the key.
>
> The keys are generated fresh, regularly, so even if someone could break the encryption tomorrow, they couldn't go back and read what you sent yesterday — a property called forward secrecy.
>
> Voice and video calls work the same way. Each call sets up its own encrypted channel between the two devices. The audio and video never sit on a server — they travel directly, peer-to-peer wherever possible, encrypted the entire trip.
>
> Even your account itself is private. Because there's no phone number and no email, there's nothing on our side that links a username to a real person.
>
> We don't sell ads. We don't sell data. We literally couldn't if we wanted to.

## Chapter 6 — The Experience (4:30 – 5:20)

**Visual:** Quick walkthrough of the actual UI — open app, tap a chat, type, send, voice note, video call, end call. Smooth screen-to-screen transitions. Calm pace.

**Voiceover:**
> VeilChat is supposed to feel like a messenger, not a security textbook.
>
> You open it. You see your chats. You tap one. You type. You send. It feels exactly like the messenger you're used to — because privacy should be invisible. The work happens underneath, in the cryptography, in the architecture, in the things you don't see.
>
> The interface is calm — soft greens, gentle typography, no notification anxiety, no engagement traps. There are no algorithms deciding what you see. There's no feed. There are just your conversations, with the people you chose, in the order you had them.

---

## Chapter 7 — Honest Analysis (5:20 – 6:00)

**Visual:** Split screen. Left column titled *"What VeilChat does well"* with green checkmarks. Right column titled *"What we're still working on"* with neutral dots. Items animate in as they're spoken.

**Voiceover:**
> Let's be honest about where we are today.
>
> What VeilChat does well: end-to-end encryption is on by default; the codebase is open source under AGPL-3.0, so you can verify every claim; you don't need to hand over any personal information to use it; and the design respects your attention.
>
> What we're still working on: the network is small — invite your closest people first. We don't yet have everything the megapps have, like full-blown payment integrations or sticker marketplaces. We're a small team, so features ship deliberately, not constantly. And like every privacy-first project, our growth depends on people choosing to switch — and asking the people they love to switch with them.

**Voiceover:**
> VeilChat is free, forever. It's open. It's verifiable. And it's built for the people you actually trust.
>
> Take your conversations back. Visit VeilChat — or get the app from the link below — and message privately, the way it should have always been.

**End card. Soft fade.**
