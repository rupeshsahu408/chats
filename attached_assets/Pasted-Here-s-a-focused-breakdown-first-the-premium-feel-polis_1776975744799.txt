Here's a focused breakdown — first the "premium feel" polish that turns a free-feeling app into a SaaS-grade one, then the differentiators that actually make people switch away from WhatsApp. Part 1 — The "premium feel" layer (perception, not features) These are the small things that make users say "this app feels expensive": Motion language. Spring-based transitions for sheets, page changes, and message bubbles entering the thread. Every tap should have a reaction within 80 ms (haptic on mobile, micro-scale on desktop). Typography & density. One serif/display face for headings, a tight-tracked sans for UI, and a second weight for timestamps. WhatsApp uses one weight everywhere — that's why it feels "utility." A real design system. Tokens for color, radius, elevation, motion. Two themes (light + true OLED black), plus an accent-color picker per user. Sound + haptic identity. A short, distinctive 3-note send/receive sound (Slack-tier). Match haptics on iOS. Empty states with personality. Illustrated, not blank. First-run, no-contacts, no-internet, archived-empty — each is a chance to charm. Composer polish. Animated placeholder rotator, slash-command palette (/poll, /schedule, /secret), rich paste handling (paste a URL → auto link card, paste an image → auto-upload). Loading == presence. Skeleton bubbles instead of spinners. Optimistic everything. Avatars. Auto-generate gradient mesh avatars from user IDs when there's no photo (Linear/Notion style). They look intentional, never "missing." A status bar that earns its space. Connection state, encryption indicator, "ratchet healthy" dot — quiet but visible. Users feel safer when they can see the lock is real. Keyboard-first on desktop. ⌘K palette, g i for inbox, g a for archive, J/K to walk threads. Power-user feel = SaaS feel. A real onboarding. 3 swipeable cards: "encrypted by default · disappears on your terms · works offline." Then a single first-message prompt. In-app changelog + product surface. A tiny "What's new" sheet, a roadmap link, a public status page. These are the SaaS table-stakes that make people trust your app.
